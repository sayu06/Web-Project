Numbers are used a lot in Spanish everyday life.Whether you need to ask which bus to

take or are buying something in a shop,you will need to know how to use the numerical system in Russian.

In case you were wondering how to say “numbers” in Spanish, it’s números.

The word for “number” in Spanish is número.So, let's learn some números, starting with Spanish cardinal numbers!

Numbers from 11 TO 19


Numbers 11-19 To form these numbers, simply add "say" to one of the numbers from 1 to 9.

In this category, you will notice that the numbers until 15 end in “-ce.’ For these numbers,

the first part is the unit, and the second is the ten. The number 11, for example, literally means:

‘one ten is 11’ and so on.Furthermore, from 16 to 19, you will notice that all numbers have the base number as an ending.

Ex: 19: (diecinueve – ‘nueve’ means 9!)

Numbers from 31 TO 40


From the number 30 onwards, it becomes even easier to count. As you stop combining the words’ veinti+nueve’ – 29, start separating and adding a ‘y’ (and).

Formula: ten + and + units

For example: The number 36 is written down as: ‘treinta y seis.’(10+3) + (y) + (6) . Let’s continue counting!
