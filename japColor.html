Colors hold significant cultural and symbolic meanings in Japan.

COLORS IN JAPANESE

Japanese Colors

English Translation

Pronunciation


あか

RED

aka



Red (赤 aka) is a prominent color in Japanese culture, representing energy, passion, and vitality. Red is commonly used in traditional clothing, such as the iconic red and white kimono worn by brides during weddings.



ぴんく

PINK

pinku



Pink (ピンク pinku) is a popular color in Japanese fashion and culture, representing femininity, sweetness, and youthfulness. It is often used in clothing, accessories, and cosmetics marketed towards women.



みどり

GREEN

GREEN

midori



Green (緑 midori) represents nature, freshness, and growth in Japanese culture. It is often associated with spring, which is a significant season in Japanese culture due to the blooming of cherry blossoms.



くろ

Black

kuro



Black (黒 kuro) is a versatile color in Japanese culture, representing both elegance and mystery. It is commonly used in clothing, such as the black formal wear worn during funerals and other solemn occasions.



あお

Dark Blue

ao



Blue (青 ao) is another important color in Japanese culture, representing calmness, stability, and peace. Blue is often associated with water, which is a significant element in Japanese art and culture.



むらさき

Purple

murasaki



Purple (紫 murasaki) is a regal color in Japanese culture, representing nobility, luxury, and creativity. It is commonly used in traditional clothing and art, such as the purple robes worn by Japanese emperors and aristocrats.



おれんじいろ

Orange

oreji-iro



Orange is a relatively new color in Japanese culture, as it was not traditionally recognized as a distinct color in Japan. Prior to the introduction of the color orange, shades of red and yellow were used to represent similar hues.



はいいろ

Grey

hairo



The color grey is often associated with subtlety, sophistication, and humility. The Japanese word for grey is "haiiro" (灰色), which literally means "ash color."



しろ

White

shiro



White (白 shiro) symbolizes purity, cleanliness, and innocence in Japanese culture. It is often used in traditional clothing and architecture, such as the white walls and paper screens of Japanese houses.
